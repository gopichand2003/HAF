Bro. Israel Raju

Beloved in Lord,

I am from a village called LB Charla in Narasapuram Mandal, West Godavari District. I was born as the only son to Vatala Ramaswamy and Mangamma. During my childhood, we faced severe financial difficulties. In the midst of that hardship, my mother accepted the Lord Jesus Christ as her personal Savior. From that moment onward, our financial situation began to improve. I was able to continue my studies up to the degree level.

After my marriage, I began to handle our family’s financial matters. Unfortunately, I faced repeated losses and eventually fell into debt. During a particularly difficult time, I went to church for my son's naming ceremony. As soon as I entered the church, tears began to flow from my eyes—that was the moment I truly encountered God.

From 1996 to 2005, I regularly attended church in my village. However, due to our worsening financial condition, we had to sell all our property to repay our debts. I tried many different jobs, but nothing worked out. Still, I continued going to church every Sunday, praying and fasting, but I couldn't find a stable way to support my family.

During that time, while reading the Bible, Psalm 32:8 spoke to me: “I will instruct you and teach you in the way you should go.” I prayed based on that verse. It was then that God spoke to me and said, “I am giving you a job in My defense department.” When I asked the Lord where I should receive training and who was doing true ministry, He showed me Holy Army Fellowship (HAF).

In June 2005, I came to Mandavalli and entered into a time of repentance. God revealed many sins in my life. I confessed every sin, and I stayed in the church for four days, spending time in prayer.

During that time, God spoke to me about many things and revealed His will to me. In the same year, during the month of July, I joined the ministry training.

Now, by God's grace, I am serving the Lord. He has given me the privilege and honor to live for Him.

May all the glory be to God.

Greetings to all.

Yours Brother in Christ’s service,

Bro. Satyam

Beloved in Lord,

I greet you in the name of our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ.

I was born into a family that did not know God. Since my parents didn’t know Jesus Christ, I followed in the way they led me. My mother was suffering from an illness, and despite visiting many doctors, she did not recover. She even went to spirit healers, but nothing helped. Her condition worsened day by day, and eventually, we brought her back from the hospital with no hope.

At that time, a woman who knew Jesus Christ came and spoke to us about the Lord. She said that Jesus is a great God who performs miracles. She told my mother that Jesus could heal her and prayed for her. Through that prayer, my mother placed her faith in Jesus—and He healed her. She is alive to this day.

After that, my parents began attending prayer meetings, and we children went along with them. However, I was not living a righteous life. Though I went to church and sang songs, I did not live a faithful life. I watched movies, drank alcohol, ate unhealthy food, told lies, and used foul language. I was living in sin, and as I grew older, I continued in these sinful habits.

I traveled to different places, and my life became worse. I began to feel hopeless and shared my struggles with my friends. Around that time, some brothers and sisters who had been saved came to our village. They shared how their lives had changed and how they had repaid those they had wronged. I was amazed to see such transformation and thought, “Could this kind of change happen in my life?”

But I was afraid. I thought my bad habits and addictions were too strong and that I would never be able to change. The brothers and sisters said to me, “You have known the Lord since childhood. God changed our lives, and He can change yours too.” But I paid no attention to their words.

My parents, who had experienced salvation, also encouraged me, but by then, I had stopped going to prayer meetings for many years. In 2004, a servant of God, Brother Abraham, came to our village and organized meetings. The brothers were working hard in the ministry, and I reluctantly went to one of the evening meetings.

In that meeting, Brother Abraham preached about how sin brings a curse into a family. He said, “Your sin can become a curse to your family. One person’s disobedience can lead many to disobedience, and one person’s obedience can lead many to obedience.” These words pierced my heart. I wept and prayed, realizing, “Yes, I should not remain disobedient. I need to change my life.”

That evening, I experienced victory over my addictions. I began attending church regularly and continued in fellowship. One Saturday evening, I went again to the Mandavalli prayer meeting. As I stepped into the place, I felt a deep peace in my heart. That night, Brother Abraham spoke about how many rejoiced at the birth of John the Baptist. He asked, “How was your birth? Were you born to bring joy to others?”

He shared an example from the forest, where wild pigs would destroy crops. To stop them, farmers would place a sharp knife coated with a substance that attracted the pigs. As the pigs licked it, they would unknowingly drink their own blood and eventually die. Brother Abraham explained that Satan deceives young people in the same way—leading them to their destruction while making them believe they are enjoying life.

This example struck me. I realized that I was like one of those wild pigs—living in sin, unaware that I was destroying myself. I wept and prayed, saying, “Lord, I want to change. I want my life to bring joy to others.”

The servants of God who came to our village encouraged me to receive salvation. I decided to surrender my life to the Lord. For about 15 days, I spent time in prayer at Mandavalli, seeking God’s presence. During that time, God revealed many areas in my life where I had gone wrong—especially how I had treated my mother, my sister, and others. I realized I had sinned against them, and God was convicting me of my wrongdoings.

Through deep prayer and repentance, I received forgiveness from God. I reconciled with the Lord and with those I had hurt. The testimonies of the servants of God strengthened me, and by God’s grace, I received true repentance. I began to live a life that pleased God.

Through Brother Abraham’s ministry, I came to understand the love of God. I also learned the importance of marriage, food, and clothing—all of which God provided without dowry or expense. In 2008, I began serving the Lord and learned many lessons through His guidance.

In 2012, I was called into full-time ministry. During that time, the Lord strengthened me greatly. However, during the pandemic, I became seriously ill. The church and my fellowship group prayed earnestly for me, and God provided the financial support needed for my treatment. Many people thought I would not survive, but God preserved my life.

Now, I am living by faith and trusting in the Lord daily. My family and I continue to walk with Him.

I humbly request your prayers—that I may continue to serve the Lord faithfully and live in a way that brings glory to His name. I thank God for His mercy and for the love and support of my dear brothers and sisters in Christ.

Greetings to all.

Yours Brother in Christ’s service,

Bro. Mojesh

My parents had three children—two older sisters and myself, the youngest. Tragedy struck early in our lives when both my mother and father passed away when I was just five years old. We were then lovingly raised by my maternal grandaunt, my mother’s sister, who had no children of her own. She took us in and cared for us as if we were her own.

During that time, a Christian sister told us, “If you believe in the Lord Jesus, she will be healed.” Desperate for hope, and despite opposition from our relatives, we visited a church for the very first time. My sister was baptized soon after. However, she passed away shortly afterward. From that point on, we stopped going to the church.

Due to financial difficulties, I had to stop my education and started working as a Masonry. As I grew older, my thoughts and desires had already become deeply corrupted.I became addicted to alcohol and immoral living. Outwardly, I appeared cheerful and made others laugh, but inwardly, I had no peace , lacked joy. I couldn’t sleep at night. Whenever I was alone, I was haunted by tormenting thoughts —“Why am I alive? What is the purpose of my life?” I was desperately searching for peace.

It was then that evangelist, sent by the Lord, visited our area from Mandavalli.One of our relatives had a repentance and came to faith, and Inspired by her testimony, both my elder sister and my mother's sister also repented and came to faith. They invited Evangelist to our home, and they preached the gospel to me many times, But I had no interest in listening, as I did not like being associated with anyone who knew the Lord Jesus.

Most of my friends were Christians. While they attended church on Sundays, they also accompanied me to bars and immoral places. This hypocrisy deeply disappointed me and led me to despise Christians—and even Christ Himself. Yet, I was still searching for peace. During my travels for work, I observed many people and came to the conclusion that having plenty of money would bring me peace. From that moment on, I became consumed with the desire to earn wealth.

I began placing cricket bets. Starting with just ₹1,000, I quickly earned ₹50,000 in a week. I dreamt of making ₹10 lakhs, investing it, and living comfortably without ever working again. But as quickly as the money came, it vanished. In the hope of winning again, I borrowed money from many people and continued gambling—but I lost everything. Around that time, my elder sister and mother's sister began attending retreats in Mandavalli. Meanwhile, I also lost ₹2 lakhs that I had saved at home to build a house. Eventually, I even pawned the gold we had in the house and lost that too.

In utter despair, I decided to end my life. I swallowed twenty sleeping pills and slashed the veins in both my wrists six times with a blade.Yet, I did not die. I tried to hang myself from the ceiling fan, but the fan broke and I fell to the ground—still, I survived .

Determined to end my life, I went to Rajahmundry with the intention of jumping into the Godavari River from the bridge. Just as I was about to jump, my mother's sister called. I thought I would say a final word before ending it all, so I answered the call and shared everything with her. She pleaded with me not to go through with it. Because of her heartfelt cries, I returned home.

Despite several attempts to live righteously, my addictions only grew worse. One day, having read Bro.Abraham testimony book, I came to Mandavalli with a desire to repent. Yet, I was still doubtful, wondering whether God would truly speak to me.

One night while I was praying, the Lord brought something to my remembrance: A long time ago, I used to go to work with my elder brother and brother-in-law. My brother had fallen in love with a woman and eventually married her. He lived with them along with her mother, who was paralyzed and bedridden. One day, while we were drinking, he expressed his frustration, saying that her presence was becoming a burden. At that moment, I took it upon myself to put her on a train bound for Odisha. We placed her on the train at Tadepalligudem and got off ourselves at Rajahmundry.

I had completely forgotten that incident, but that night the Lord brought it back to my memory. He made me realize, 'This is the kind of person you are.' Overwhelmed with conviction, I broke down in tears and confessed every sin in prayer.During that moment of repentance, I heard a voice say, “Isaiah 43:5.” When I opened my eyes, everyone was asleep. I rushed to Evangelist and told them what happened. They showed me the verse in the Bible. I had never read the Bible or attended church before—this was no illusion.

It was the Lord Himself speaking to me. That day, I experienced the true peace and joy that only God can give. By the grace of God, I later got married and am now serving the Lord in ministry, following His divine calling.

Dear brothers and sisters, I humbly ask for your prayers as I continue in His service.

Bro. Sathyanandam

I live in a small village called Lakshmipuram in Krishna District. My wife and I used to attend the church regularly. After some time, God blessed us with two sons. Though we continued attending church, there was no real change in my life. I still carried my old habits. After some time, our pastor baptized both my wife and me, but even then, my life remained unchanged.

My wife and I used to travel ten kilometers from our village to attend church, and we often quarrel with each other during the bus journey. I had no peace of mind. We used to attend gatherings in nearby villages and towns. As I listened to the preaching of the Word, I began to feel a deep sense of conviction and even disgust toward myself.

I was in a constant state of struggle, and there seemed to be no change in my life. One day, I decided to fast and pray for 40 days, believing it might bring some change. There was a church in our village where they were observing fasting days, so I joined them. I spent 40 days without food, but I didn’t know how to pray or how to confess my sins. I would simply pray, "Lord, bless me, bless me, bless me."

After completing the 40 days, I came out of the church, but instead of finding peace, I felt even more broken. I still had no peace, joy, or rest. I couldn’t sleep at night. My thoughts were filled with worry about my life and my family. God began to make me understand my family’s situation. My father was a heavy drinker who never took care of our household. From my childhood, I had been in emotional turmoil, crying every day.

God showed me that my children would experience the same suffering if I didn’t change. That night, I cried out to God with tears, praying that my children would not have to go through the same painful experiences I had. I realized then that even if I earn nothing else in this world, it would be enough if I could pass on godliness and righteousness to my children.

I wanted to walk in the right path and live a life of true devotion. I searched for the right way and consulted many pastors. I continued attending church regularly. Together with some youth in our village, we decided to organize revival meetings. We invited pastors from faraway places and held three-day meetings. We did this for three years. Yet, I still could not find the spiritual path I was longing for.

By that time, some people from our village had attended the ‘Holy Army Fellowship’ and experienced repentance. They came to me and shared, ‘We have been going to church for 15 years and thought we were leading a devotional life. But when we went to Mandavalli and heard from Brother Abraham, we realized we needed true repentance. While staying there, God revealed our sins like a film played before our eyes. We confessed everything, and God spoke to us. We felt a joy we had never known.’

They encouraged me to come and experience that same transformation. I asked many questions, and they answered with great love and patience. Even when I spoke harshly, their words left a deep impression on me. They would occasionally visit me and speak with me, and they gave me a spiritual magazine published through the Evangelist, Brother Abraham.

As I was reading that magazine, a verse from Psalm 45:7 strengthened me. I didn’t take their words seriously at first, but the verse in the magazine deeply touched me. Based on that, my wife and I also went to Mandavalli and repented. I confessed my sins, and God spoke to me. After joining the Holy Army Fellowship, I received joy and peace.

Through the church in that village, Evangelist Brother Solomon came. From then on, we began attending church regularly. He used to take us to Mandavalli, and Brother Abraham would spend time speaking with us, teaching us faith and devotion.

As we continued to grow in God and walked in the right path, one day my younger son, who was three years old at the time, suffered a severe seizure and became stiff. When the doctors tried to give him an injection, it wouldn’t go in. Eventually, they told us there was nothing more they could do and left.

Dear ones, at that moment, I didn’t know what to do. Based on the thought that God gave me, I tearfully called Brother Abraham and explained what had happened. He encouraged me, prayed, and said, “Go home; your son will get up and sit.” I rushed home, and I saw my son sitting up. That brought me great happiness. Many such miracles have happened in my life.

During this time, my wife also started becoming weak. We went to the hospital where, after a scan revealed lumps in her breast, they diagnosed her with breast cancer. They suggested surgery to remove the lumps but said they couldn't guarantee the outcome. I was shattered and filled with sorrow.

In that time of prayer and despair, we went to Brother Abraham in Mandavalli and explained everything about my wife's condition. He was deeply moved and said, “At such a young age, such a disease,” and he comforted us with the promises of God from Matthew 10:1–9. He gave us hope, saying that if we pray with faith, God would remove the disease. As we continued praying, her health began to improve.

God continued to perform miracles, and day by day, we received new joy. Later, Brother Abraham, having fulfilled the Lord's call, went to be with the Lord. I cried for many days. I couldn’t find comfort from that grief. Whether my eyes were open or closed, I could see Brother Abraham in front of me.

After many days, I understood that Brother Abraham would not return. I became angry with God and felt like giving up on God. At that time, Sister Rani Abraham sent some believers and Evangelists to our area from the fellowship. They said, “Brother Abraham faithfully received promises, established the fellowship, ministered powerfully in a short time, and entered the Kingdom of Heaven according to God’s will.”

With the desire to live faithfully for God, I began spending more time with the pastor serving in our area. During this time, God began to speak to me and called me into ministry. I thought I was already serving alongside the pastors, but God spoke to me three times. The third time, He told me clearly to go for training in Mandavalli.

That word scared me because I had no education, I grew up in a village, and I didn’t know how to speak with people. Still, I had the desire to serve. But because I had many debts, I thought I should first clear them and then go for ministry. Due to famine in our area, I decided to buy an auto to earn and pay off the debts.

That night, God told me, “Do not buy an auto. You will suffer loss, and many others will suffer with you.” But I did not listen to God. I followed my own understanding and took more loans to buy the auto. I gave the vehicle to one person, and he had an accident. Then I gave it to another person, and he too had an accident. My debts increased even more.

I had to put aside my Bible and drive the auto myself. As time went on and the debts were nearly repaid, a weak thought entered my heart—to stay with the pastor and continue driving the auto to make a living. Immediately, I met with an accident. Everyone on the road was injured and lying in pain.

I raised my hands to heaven and cried, “Lord, it’s because of me—please forgive me.” By God’s grace, no one lost their life. But then, another accident happened. At that moment, I could no longer bear the shame. I thought of ending my life.

The fellowship, the Evangelist, and the church people supported me with great love. The Lord Himself spoke to me saying, “Do not be afraid, be courageous.” Those words filled me with strength and boldness. I sold the auto, cleared all my debts, and went to training in Mandavalli. Spiritual mother and the sisters there encouraged me and helped me stand firm.

By the grace of God, I am now continuing in the Lord’s ministry. I humbly request you all to pray earnestly for me—to remain faithful to the fellowship and to the Lord.

Greetings to all.

Yours Brother in Christ’s service,
